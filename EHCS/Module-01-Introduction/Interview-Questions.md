# Beginner Level

## 1. What is Data?

**Answer:**

Data is a collection of raw, unprocessed facts or figures that have no meaningful context by themselves. It can be in the form of text, numbers, images, audio, or videos.

---

## 2. What is Information?

**Answer:**

Information is processed and organized data that has meaning and helps in decision-making.

---

## 3. Differentiate between Data and Information.

| Data | Information |
|------|-------------|
| Raw facts | Processed facts |
| No meaning | Meaningful |
| Input | Output |
| Unorganized | Organized |

---

## 4. What is Information Security?

**Answer:**

Information Security (InfoSec) is the practice of protecting information from unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, destruction, or disruption while ensuring Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.

---

## 5. What is Cyber Security?

**Answer:**

Cyber Security is the practice of protecting computers, networks, applications, and digital information from cyber attacks and unauthorized access.

---

## 6. What is the difference between Information Security and Cyber Security?

| Information Security | Cyber Security |
|----------------------|----------------|
| Protects all forms of information | Protects digital systems |
| Covers physical and digital data | Focuses only on digital assets |
| Broader concept | Subset of Information Security |

---

## 7. What is an Asset?

**Answer:**

An asset is anything valuable to an individual or organization that requires protection.

Examples:

- Customer Database
- Source Code
- Laptop
- Cloud Server

---

## 8. What is a Threat?

**Answer:**

A threat is any event or action that has the potential to damage an asset.

Examples:

- Malware
- Fire
- Insider Attack
- Flood

---

## 9. What is a Vulnerability?

**Answer:**

A vulnerability is a weakness in a system that can be exploited by a threat.

Example:

An outdated web server running unpatched software.

---

## 10. What is Risk?

**Answer:**

Risk is the possibility of loss when a threat exploits a vulnerability.

Formula:

Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact

---

## 11. What is the CIA Triad?

**Answer:**

The CIA Triad consists of:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

These are the three core principles of Information Security.

---

## 12. Explain Confidentiality with an example.

**Answer:**

Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible only to authorized users.

Example:

Only HR employees can access salary records.

---

## 13. Explain Integrity with an example.

**Answer:**

Integrity ensures that information remains accurate and is not modified without authorization.

Example:

Changing examination marks illegally violates integrity.

---

## 14. Explain Availability with an example.

**Answer:**

Availability ensures that systems and data remain accessible whenever authorized users need them.

Example:

A banking website should remain available 24×7.

---

## 15. What is Authenticity?

**Answer:**

Authenticity verifies that a user, device, or message is genuine.

Example:

OTP verification during login.

---

## 16. What is Non-Repudiation?

**Answer:**

Non-Repudiation ensures that a sender cannot deny sending a message and a receiver cannot deny receiving it.

Digital Signatures are commonly used to achieve Non-Repudiation.

---

## 17. What is Hacking?

**Answer:**

Hacking is the process of identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities in computer systems.

Hacking can be legal or illegal depending on authorization.

---

## 18. Who is a Hacker?

**Answer:**

A hacker is a person with technical knowledge who identifies or exploits vulnerabilities in computer systems.

---

## 19. What is Ethical Hacking?

**Answer:**

Ethical Hacking is the authorized process of identifying and reporting vulnerabilities before malicious attackers exploit them.

---

## 20. What is the difference between Hacking and Ethical Hacking?

| Hacking | Ethical Hacking |
|---------|-----------------|
| May be illegal | Always authorized |
| Malicious intent possible | Defensive purpose |
| Causes damage | Improves security |

---

## 21. Explain White Hat, Black Hat, and Grey Hat Hackers.

| White Hat | Grey Hat | Black Hat |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Authorized | No permission but limited malicious intent | Unauthorized and malicious |
| Legal | Usually illegal | Illegal |
| Improves security | Mixed | Causes damage |

---

## 22. Why do organizations hire Ethical Hackers?

**Answer:**

Organizations hire Ethical Hackers to:

- Find vulnerabilities
- Prevent cyber attacks
- Improve security
- Meet compliance requirements
- Protect customer data

---

## 23. What is Information Warfare?

**Answer:**

Information Warfare is the strategic use of information and communication systems to influence or gain an advantage over an opponent.

---

## 24. What is Cyber Warfare?

**Answer:**

Cyber Warfare refers to cyber attacks conducted by one nation or organization against another to disrupt critical infrastructure or steal sensitive information.

---

## 25. Name some career opportunities in Cyber Security.

**Answer:**

- Ethical Hacker
- Penetration Tester
- SOC Analyst
- Security Analyst
- Malware Analyst
- Digital Forensics Analyst
- Application Security Engineer
- Cloud Security Engineer
